History
1950's to the 21st Century

                    1950 to 1980

The 1950's: A number of inventors started entertaining the idea of a portable electronic object.

1971: The Carte Bleue was equipped with a magnetic strip that allowed the use of a secret code.

1974: The dream of creating a smart card comes true with Roland Moreno's invention.

1979: Smart cards become the global standard.


              1981 to 2018

1987: First large-scale smart card application implemented in the United States
with the U.S. Department of Agriculture's nationwide Peanut Marketing Card.

1998: Microsoft announced its new Windows smart card operating system.

2009: Contactless payment system infrastructure implemented in the US.

2012: Swift Card for West Midlands Transport Introduced.

2015: Swift Card start rolling out, starting with the rail network and expanding
to the National Express bus operators, who started to accept pay-as-you-go
Swift Cards in September.

2018: At the Google conference, it was announced that Swift cards would be
integrated with Google Pay to allow transport users to purchase travel cards
online and have it available on mobile phones via Android.
